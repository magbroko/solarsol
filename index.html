Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about solar in Nigeria — rainy season, AC loads, Band A billing, and more.

Yes. Solar panels still generate power on cloudy and rainy days — typically 15–25% of peak output. Our systems are sized for Nigeria's average 5.5 peak sun hours, and we factor in seasonal variation. Battery storage ensures you have power through extended cloudy periods and at night.

Absolutely. We size systems for surge loads (pumps and fridges have high startup currents) and continuous loads like ACs. Our ROI calculator lets you select priority appliances — we recommend a minimum 3.5KVA inverter if you run a pumping machine. Hybrid systems can run ACs, pumps, and essential loads with battery backup.

Band A is the highest electricity tariff tier (≈₦225/kWh) with up to 20 hours of supply. Solar ROI compares your current Disco spend (including Band A/B/C rates) against the upfront cost of a solar system. Our calculator factors in Band A tariffs, generator fuel savings, and payback — so you see how quickly solar pays for itself.
